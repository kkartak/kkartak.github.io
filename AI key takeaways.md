# AI: Is it beneficial for writers, or is it taking away from individual creativity?

## Can it be Useful without removing effort from the equation?

The biggest benefit of AI for many people is the ability to remove the work and effort of doing the outline, the draft, and the final draft. Instead, they can plug in data or facts they've looked up and ask it to make all of those things for them. It makes the process of writing so much easier and less stressful for many people, but it also denies them the opportunity to come up with something original and in their own words. My hope is that as AI evolves, the way it helps people becomes more refined and specific, like the study-and-learn method co-pilot offers; this allows people to still get guidance from AI without it doing all the thinking and work for them. It gives you advice on what you could do and how to go about it, rather than just giving you the answer. I would like to see more AI companies and software adopt this model and disregard the alternative of just providing answers. If you need a question answered, use a web browser and find it for yourself.


## Drawbacks of co-authoring


Often, the AI gets the bigger picture of the request I've asked it to generate information for me; however, it often gets the small details — the important facts I am looking for — wrong. That can be a major drawback in terms of reliability, its branded as being a time saver when if the fine details are wrong I have to spend more time ensuring that every little detail down to the citation is correct when I could've done the research and writing on my own and been done much faster as I was the one to confirm the facts and write them out exactly as they are presented.
