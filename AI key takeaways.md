# AI: Is it beneficial for writers, or is it taking away from individual creativity?

## Can it be Useful without removing effort from the equation?

The biggest benefit of AI for many people is the ability to remove the work and effort of doing the outline, the draft, and the final draft. Instead, they can plug in data or facts they've looked up and ask it to make all of those things for them. It makes the writing process much easier and less stressful for many people, but it also denies them the opportunity to come up with something original and in their own words. My hope is that as AI evolves, the way it helps people becomes more refined and specific, like the study-and-learn method co-pilot offers, allowing people to still get guidance from AI without it doing all the thinking and work for them. It gives you advice on what you could do and how to go about it, rather than just giving you the answer. I would like to see more AI companies and software adopt this model and disregard the alternative of just providing answers. If you need a question answered, use a web browser and find it for yourself.


## Drawbacks of co-authoring


Often, the AI gets the bigger picture of the request I've asked it to generate information for me; however, it often gets the small details — the important facts I am looking for — wrong. That can be a major drawback in terms of reliability, its branded as being a time saver when if the fine details are wrong I have to spend more time ensuring that every little detail down to the citation is correct when I could've done the research and writing on my own and been done much faster as I was the one to confirm the facts and write them out exactly as they are presented.

## How do I feel about AI: What might I use it for

I am, for the most part, relatively indifferent about AI. I don't mind if people love it and want to see it advance and be used, or others who think it is dangerous and harming the planet with its massive data centers. Every advancement in human history has been met with resistance and a lack of rules and polices in its infancy. The only way to improve it, without taking away from people's creativity and ability to think for themselves, is the biggest concern for AI now. Some, not all, rely too heavily on this algorithmic computer to provide all the answers and effort for them, and I agree that it is dangerous; however, this is only because of the lack of regulation within the industry, and I think we need to take it more seriously than we do in that regard.

Again, I've said this before, but I really only use AI when I want its advice on a fictional story I've been writing, like generating an image based on the text I wrote and added to the database, or some made-up city or nation's name, and then tweaking it to make it my own. I don't use it regularly unless I want input on something that's already been written, because I've already put in the work and find no issue, and I don't want a peer review from someone who will be very blunt and critical.

### Closing Remark

I believe AI has a place within society today; we just need to better regulate what it's allowed to do, save, and use as data. It shouldn't be allowed within acedamic spaces unless it is being used as a study tool not a scapegoat to doing the work, and finally for me personally I do not need it when I am on a web browser attempting to look things up myslef I don't want a data center wasting resources by building a prompt answer for me, part of the fun of research is looking for it yourself not having it handed to you on a silver platter. I'm not built that way; it takes away all the joy and drive I had.
